Pay-Per-Click

PPC (pay-per-click) marketing is a form of online advertising in which advertisers bid on keywords to have their ads presented to a specific audience, platform, or keyword search. These ads are extremely relevant because end-users are searching for a specific product, service, or information, and your ad is triggered because it meets certain criteria.

For PPC on Google, set up your Google ads account. You can also invite other users. Once the google ads account is set up, link it with your google analytics. Within your Google Ads account, you will get a piece of tracking code that you will need to add to your website. For proper working of this code, you will need access to the backend of your website to insert it at the proper location.

When you first begin to structure your accounts, you will start by creating campaigns that are focused on keyword themes. Ad Groups should be organized by keyword variations. This allows you to break down your target focus to specific products you offer. It’s crucial to only have one theme of keywords per ad group so the ads are directly related to the keywords. Negative keywords can be used to remove unqualified traffic from clicking on your ads. This will allow you to pay for ad clicks that are more relevant to your business.

You can create audiences based on specific actions users take with your site. You can create these segments based upon specific page views, time spent on site, pages per visit, and more. Audiences can also be bid upon based on relevance. At the end of the day, there are hundreds of different audiences that can be created to suit your needs and drive the most relevant traffic and conversions for your site.
